Increasing Volunteer Recruitment

Context

Million Peacemakers (MP) is a non-profit organization that trains people on how to peacefully resolve conflicts using the Nonflict way. Their recent focus has been their sub-organization, Million Peacemakers Youth (MPY), which focuses on training young adults specifically.

Client: Million Peacemakers
Project type: Non-profit landing page
Team: 2 UX/UI Designers (including myself), WIX developer (me), Art Director, Design Lead
Timeline: 8 weeks ~ Q4 2022

The Problem

Million Peacemakers didn’t have a dedicated place to promote their growing sub-organization, MPY. Since they couldn’t find the information they needed, potential youth volunteers left the website without joining the cause.


The Objective

Research, strategize, and design an experience to help young adults understand the Nonflict way, and the benefits of joining MPY, converting them to youth volunteers and workshop participants.

The Process

Empathize

We lacked understanding about MP’s needs and users, and without a budget for user research, we surveyed the client to uncover who their key audiences are and what their goals are for the coming years.

Client Goals

  • Recruit more youth volunteers to help run their increasing number of workshops.
  • Multiply participation in youth workshops.
  • Create exposure, traffic, and momentum for Million Peacemakers Youth.

Conceptualize

After benchmarking landing page structures of popular non-profit websites, we found their best methods of conversion were through incorporating social proof, short and sweet breakdowns, genuine imagery, and approachable calls-to-action, and used those insights to create a wireframe.

Design

With an 8 week timeline, we had to work fast to sell the client on our concept, reducing rounds of feedback and preventing any delays in delivery. We created a stylescape—a collaged mood board of sorts—which presented colours, website elements, icons, and photography as a holistic vision for the client to greenlight before moving onto a mock-up. Please turn on your volume for all videos to listen to the rationale.

Deliver

Without a budget for developers, we took this as an opportunity to maximize the capabilities of design and upskill by learning how to use a site builder. Additionally, the new visual identity had to be transferable to their main site, and sustainable after handoff, so I worked in WIX to implement a responsive design with transferable components.

Eye-catching Hero 

The flowing line draws a user’s attention from the headline to the captivating image of a young volunteer.

Short and Sweet Breakdowns 

To prevent overwhelming users, important information about MPY’s culture, method of conflict resolution, impact, and benefits of joining are broken down into easily digestible sections.

Persuading With Social Proof

Testimonials and youth stories are front and center to inspire site visitors while giving them an idea of what to expect from volunteering.

Approachable Calls-To-Action

We start with low commitment CTAs to build up to the main CTA, which asks users to become a youth volunteer. A distinct blue section further down the page highlights the benefits of joining, but the CTA button is also in the navigation menu for easy access at all times.

Opportunities to Engage

The client emphasized that the contact form generated numerous opportunities on their main site, so it was crucial to include it on the youth site too.

Styleguide

The components I created in WIX are easy to use, customize, and transfer, empowering the client to continue building autonomously. MP has already rebranded their main site and social media accordingly.

Reflection

“An educated assumption is still a valid reason.” That’s what my UX lead said to me when I asked how I should justify design decisions without a budget to conduct research with primary users. The constraint compelled me to be resourceful by benchmarking existing non-profit websites and making the most of information provided by the client.
